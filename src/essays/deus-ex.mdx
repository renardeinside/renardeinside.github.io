# Des ex intelligentia

Recently I've been sitting in one of the Berlin bars on a small party with developers and ML engineers. Indeed, most of us - working in the field of AI - are not only interested in the technical aspects of our work, but also in the philosophical implications of the technology we are building.
After discussing such important topics as:

* Weather in Berlin (which deemed to be quite rainy)
* The best place to eat in Berlin (which is a very subjective topic)
* The best place to work in Berlin (which is also subjective)

The party has kinda split into smaller subgroups, where each of them started to discuss their own stuff. One of the guys, a mid-level Java developer (let's call him further A. for brevity) approached me and we've started a casual dialogue about everything. 
After some general chit-chat, he suddenly stood and quite quietly asked me:

> Do you think there is a chance that *we* (developers) will be replaced by AI?

I've heard this question many times before (sometimes in more of a joke form), but this time it was different. The guy was not joking, he was _actually concerned about his future_.
